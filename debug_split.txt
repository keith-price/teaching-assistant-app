**3. TEXT**

Read the article about a recent incident in Iran.

---

**Iran School and Military Base Hit Multiple Times, Satellite Images Show**

**1. The Incident and Its Immediate Impact**
**Satellite imagery analysis** reveals that a school and a nearby military base in southern Iran were struck multiple times. Iranian authorities reported that the attack killed 168 people on Saturday. Videos and satellite images show **extensive damage** around the Shajareh Tayebeh primary school in Minab and the adjacent Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) compound. Experts suggest the area was "struck by multiple **simultaneous** or near-simultaneous strikes." One building within the IRGC base was completely destroyed, and the school building was **partially collapsed**. Footage from the **immediate aftermath** showed panic, with families searching for victims in the rubble. Later, aerial footage showed at least 100 freshly dug graves.

**2. Blame and Verification Challenges**
Iranian officials have blamed the US and Israel for the attack, but neither country has accepted responsibility. Israel stated it was not aware of operations in the area, while the US Defense Secretary said Washington was investigating and would "never target civilian targets." There has been **intense speculation** about the intended target of the attack. An ongoing internet blackout in Iran has made it difficult to **independently verify** details of the incident.

**3. What Satellite Imagery Reveals**
Satellite imagery captured days after the incident revealed **widespread destruction**, showing more damage than initially seen in videos. Several buildings were partially or fully destroyed, with at least five showing visible **impact craters** and black scorch marks, confirming multiple strikes. Analysts noted that so many impact sites close together suggest one or more targets were intentionally hit in the area, though the specific intent remains unclear. The school building is located near the IRGC base. Historically, satellite images from 2013 show the school as part of the same compound, but a wall was built in 2016 to separate them.

**4. The Victims**
Iranian officials stated that most of the 168 people killed were children. While these details could not be independently verified, a handwritten list published by Iranian media included the names of 56 people reportedly killed, with 48 of them aged between six and 11. Photos and videos showed child-sized coffins at funerals, and crowds carrying pictures of boys and girls.

    **Satellite imagery analysis** has revealed **extensive damage** to a school and a military base in southern Iran, suggesting multiple strikes occurred. **3. TEACHING NOTES**

*   **Differentiation:**
    *   **Support:** For students struggling with vocabulary, allow them to work in pairs for the matching activity. Provide sentence frames for the production task, e.g., "The article describes how __________. There was __________, and it's difficult to __________. Neither side has __________, leading to _________."
    *   **Challenge:** Encourage stronger students to research the concept of "information warfare" or "propaganda" related to international incidents and discuss how this article fits (or doesn't fit) into that context. In the production task, ask them to write a short paragraph from the perspective of an official from one of the blamed countries.

*   **CCQs (Concept Checking Questions) for "independently verify":**
    1.  If a news report says "A local official stated X," but you can't find any other sources saying the same thing, has the information been *independently verified*? (No, because there is only one source, and it might be biased.)
    2.  If an international organization sends its own experts to investigate an incident and then publishes a report, has the information been *independently verified*? (Yes, because they are checking it themselves, separately from any involved parties.)
